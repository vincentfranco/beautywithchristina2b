Cavitation Treatment

Cavitation treatment uses ultrasound waves to disrupt the cell membranes of fat cells, causing them to break down and release their contents into the interstitial space. These damaged cells are then naturally processed and eliminated by the body.

A single session typically lasts between 30 to 45 minutes, depending on the area being treated. It's recommended to have a 7-day interval between sessions to allow the body to process the fat cells effectively.

Cryolipolysis Treatment

Cryolipolysis, also known as "fat freezing," involves cooling fat cells to a temperature that causes them to crystallize and die. Over the following months, the body naturally eliminates these dead cells, resulting in fat reduction.

Each session takes about 35 to 60 minutes, and most people require a series of three treatments for optimal results. The full extent of fat reduction becomes noticeable after about 2 to 4 months.

Radio Frequency Treatment

Radio Frequency (RF) treatment heats the deeper layers of the skin, stimulating collagen and elastin production. This process tightens the skin and improves its texture, complementing other fat reduction treatments.

Treatment duration varies, but typically ranges from 15 to 40 minutes per area. It's important to allow time for the skin to recover between sessions.

Dermology Treatment

Dermology treatment uses skin rolling technology to break down fat pockets and reduce cellulite. The vacuum suction and rollers help to drain away the fat cells, leaving the skin smoother and more toned.

Sessions usually last around 30 to 45 minutes, and similar to other treatments, it's recommended to have a recovery period between sessions.
